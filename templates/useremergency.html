Safety Procedures

  1. Fire: If you smell smoke or see fire, exit the building immediately and call the Fire Department.
  2. Medical Emergency: Call the hospital or emergency hotline and provide details about the situation.
  3. Intruder: Lock all doors and windows, and contact the Police Department immediately.
  4. Natural Disasters: Follow local authorities' instructions and move to a safe location.

Important Note!

Ensure that you are familiar with the emergency exits in your building. Participate in regular safety drills and keep your emergency contacts updated.

Emergency Kit Checklist

Step-by-Step preparing your Emergency Kit

    "Preparedness is the key to resilience, ensuring you're ready for the unexpected with the tools and resources needed to protect yourself and your loved ones."

  1. Step 1: Gather Non-Perishable Food (3 days' supply): Canned goods, dry food, and packaged meals. Rotate periodically.
  2. Step 2: Prepare Water Supply (3 days' supply): 1 gallon per person per day. Use clean containers or bottled water.
  3. Step 3: Assemble a First Aid Kit: Include bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, and personal medications.
  4. Step 4: Gather a Flashlight and Extra Batteries: Pack an LED flashlight and extra batteries. Consider a hand-crank option.
  5. Step 5: Battery-Powered or Hand-Crank Radio: Ensure it can receive emergency broadcasts.
  6. Step 6: Organize Medications: Pack a week's supply of daily meds and store in a labeled organizer.
  7. Step 7: Collect Important Documents: Store ID, insurance papers, and contacts in a waterproof container.
  8. Step 8: Pack Warm Clothing and Blankets: Include layers, sturdy shoes, and thermal blankets.
  9. Final Step: Pack Everything in a Portable Bag: Pack everything in a portable bag and check supplies every 6-12 months.
